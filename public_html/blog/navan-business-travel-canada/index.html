What is Navan?

Navan is a corporate travel and expense (T&E) platform - the product formerly known as TripActions. Instead of one app for booking trips, a separate corporate card, a spreadsheet for receipts, and a manual expense-report process at month-end, Navan rolls all of it into a single system. Travel that gets booked in Navan flows straight into expenses, card transactions reconcile themselves, and finance sees spend as it happens rather than weeks later.

Navan in Canada

Navan operates globally and supports Canadian businesses, with multi-currency handling so you can run travel and expenses in CAD. As with any cross-border platform, the practical questions worth confirming before you commit are sales-tax handling on the expense side, which corporate-card options are available to Canadian entities, and how the accounting sync maps to your bookkeeping setup. Those details change, so check them against your own stack on Navan’s site.

Who should use it

  • Companies with regular business travel - staff flying to clients, conferences, or other offices.
  • Finance teams tired of chasing receipts and reconciling cards by hand at month-end.
  • Growing teams that have outgrown personal cards and a shared spreadsheet for expenses.
  • Businesses that want spend controls enforced at booking time instead of caught after the money’s gone.

If your travel is mostly rides around the city, a corporate ride-hailing tool is the simpler fit. Navan earns its place the moment airfare, hotels, and expense reports enter the picture.

Frequently asked questions

Is Navan available in Canada?

Yes. Navan serves businesses globally, including Canada, with multi-currency support so Canadian companies can manage travel and expenses in CAD and other currencies.

How much does Navan cost?

Navan’s travel booking side is free to start for most businesses. The expense-management product is priced per active user per month, with paid tiers that add expense automation, corporate cards, and deeper controls. Pricing changes, so verify the current numbers on Navan’s site before you commit.

What’s the difference between Navan and a corporate ride-hailing app?

A ride-hailing app like Hopp covers point-to-point ground transport around a city. Navan covers the whole business trip - flights, hotels, cars, and rail under company policy - plus the expense and corporate-card workflow that follows. They solve different parts of travel and are often used together.

Does Navan handle expense reports automatically?

Largely, yes. Travel booked in Navan flows straight into expenses, corporate-card transactions are matched automatically, and receipts are captured with OCR - so most trips need little or no manual expense entry.
