What “CCPC” means

A Canadian-Controlled Private Corporation (CCPC) is a private corporation that’s resident in Canada and not controlled, directly or indirectly, by non-resident persons or public corporations. Almost every Canadian small business is a CCPC. Only CCPCs get the federal Small Business Deduction.

The business limit

The federal Small Business Deduction applies to the first $500,000 of active business income. This is the “business limit.” It’s shared across associated CCPCs (the “associated group” rule) and is reduced for CCPCs with:

  • Taxable capital between $10M and $50M (phase-out).
  • Adjusted aggregate investment income above $50,000 (passive income grind).

Most provinces follow the federal $500,000 limit; Saskatchewan’s small business limit is $600,000.
