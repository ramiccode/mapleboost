Zoho Books - best overall value

Zoho Books packs a genuinely deep accounting toolkit - invoicing, quotes, bills, purchase orders, bank reconciliation, inventory, project billing, and solid reporting - at a fraction of what the older incumbents charge. There’s a free plan for solopreneurs and micro-businesses, and paid tiers start around $15/month (billed annually), so the value is hard to beat for most newly incorporated Canadian businesses.

The Canadian edition handles GST/HST, PST, and QST with automated tax tracking, helps you prepare federal and provincial sales-tax returns, and manages T4A and T5018 contractor slips. Bank feeds and reconciliation cover the major Canadian banks, and there’s Canadian-based support plus mobile and Windows desktop apps.

Pros: Outstanding value, including a real free plan. Deep features (inventory, projects, multi-currency, workflow automation). Clean, modern UX. Tight integration with the wider Zoho ecosystem (CRM, Inventory, Expense). Free assisted migration from other tools.
Cons: Fewer Canadian bookkeepers are certified on it than on the largest incumbents, so confirm your accountant is comfortable with it. You get the most value by staying inside the Zoho ecosystem. Verify current CRA filing specifics for your situation.

Xero - best for cross-border and inventory

Xero is New Zealand-built and feels it - clean information design, predictable navigation, and the strongest multi-currency support of the group. If you sell in USD or EUR, or you have meaningful inventory, Xero handles both very well.

GST/HST and QST are well-supported in the Canadian edition, with direct filing. The bookkeeper network in Canada is growing fast, especially in BC and Ontario.

Pros: Cleanest UX. Best multi-currency. Strong inventory module. Unlimited users on every plan.
Cons: Pricier than Zoho Books for comparable features. Payroll is via a partner (Wagepoint integration is excellent).

FreshBooks - best for invoicing-heavy service businesses

FreshBooks is Toronto-built and started life as an invoicing tool. It’s still the easiest tool in the group to send a clean invoice, follow up automatically, and accept payment. Accounting features have caught up over the last few years but the heart of the product is still invoicing and time tracking.

Best for consultants, freelancers, agencies, and trades - anyone whose accounting is mostly “hours billed + materials.” Less ideal for inventory or complex chart-of-accounts work.

Pros: Effortless invoicing. Built-in time tracking. Great mobile app. Canadian customer support.
Cons: Per-client pricing on lower tiers gets expensive fast. Reporting is lighter than Zoho Books or Xero.

Wave - best free option

Wave is also Toronto-built and is genuinely free for accounting, invoicing, and receipt scanning. Payroll and payment processing cost extra. It’s a solid answer for solopreneurs, side-business owners, and anyone whose books fit on one screen - though Zoho Books’ free plan is now a strong alternative with more room to grow.

Wave handles GST/HST tracking but does not file directly with CRA - you copy numbers into NETFILE or your accountant’s software. For most sole proprietors, that’s a fine trade.

Pros: Free. Genuinely Canadian. Simple. Good enough for most freelancers.
Cons: No direct CRA filing. Limited reporting. Multi-business requires separate accounts.

How to choose

  • You want the most features for the lowest cost: Zoho Books - and the free plan lets you start at $0.
  • You sell to the US or hold inventory: Xero.
  • You bill by the hour or project: FreshBooks.
  • You want a free, no-frills set of books: Wave, or Zoho Books’ free plan.

Switching costs

Migrating mid-year is painful but doable. Most providers offer assisted migration from one of the others - Zoho Books includes free migration help. The best time to switch is the start of a fiscal year; the second-best time is right after a GST/HST filing closes a period.

FAQ

Do I need separate payroll software?

Zoho Books connects to payroll through integrations; Xero offers a payroll add-on; Wave has a paid payroll product; FreshBooks does not include payroll natively. Many Canadian businesses use Wagepoint regardless of accounting choice - it integrates broadly.

Can my accountant access my file?

Yes, every option above supports accountant access (usually free for the accountant). With Zoho Books, confirm your bookkeeper is comfortable working in it before you commit.

Does Zoho Books have a free plan?

Yes. Zoho Books offers a free plan for small and micro-businesses under a revenue threshold, and a 14-day free trial on the paid tiers, so you can test it before paying anything.
