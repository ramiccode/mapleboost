How we evaluated

We graded each service on five dimensions: (1) all-in price including government fees, (2) jurisdictions supported, (3) inclusions in the base package (NUANS, minute book, share certificates, bylaws), (4) post-incorporation value (compliance reminders, annual return filing, corporate changes), and (5) responsiveness of support across email and chat.

2. LawDepot - cheapest legit option

LawDepot is the low-cost option for founders who want a guided filing flow without paying for a bundled premium package. On LawDepot’s official Canada incorporation page, the base filing fee starts at $79, standard filing is listed at 4–7 business days, and a one-business-day rush add-on is available. Best for straightforward federal, Ontario, Alberta, BC, and Saskatchewan filings where you care most about price.

Pros

  • $79 entry price on standard filings
  • NUANS and minute book sold à la carte
  • Rush filing option available

Cons

  • Base package is lean by design
  • Minute book is an add-on, not included
  • Quebec is not in the current package list

FAQ

Which incorporator is cheapest?

LawDepot is the current budget pick, starting at $79 + government filing fees on its official Canada incorporation page.

Do these services file in every province?

Ownr covers federal and all provinces. LawDepot currently lists packages for federal, Alberta, British Columbia, Ontario, and Saskatchewan. MyCorporation has active Canada incorporation and maintenance pages, but you should confirm your exact province and filing path at checkout.

Is the minute book included?

Included with Ownr. LawDepot sells minute-book style paperwork as add-ons; MyCorporation says its Canada corporation packages include sample bylaws and sample documents.

Can I switch incorporators later?

Yes - the corporation itself is filed with the government, not the service. You can move your minute book and ongoing filings to a different provider anytime.
