Business services for Canadian founders

Every Canadian small business eventually needs the same handful of services: a way to incorporate, books that don’t break at year-end, a business bank account, payroll once you hire, and a few legal templates. Here’s how we’d pick each one in 2026.

Incorporation

Online incorporators handle your NUANS name search, file your Articles of Incorporation federally or provincially, and ship a minute book. Doing it yourself is cheaper but takes a Saturday; using a service costs roughly $50–$300 over government fees.

  • Best overall: Ownr - RBC-owned, polished, runs on autopilot for the post-incorporation paperwork.
  • Best low-cost option: LawDepot - starts at $79 + government fees, with NUANS and minute book as optional add-ons.
  • Best for very lean budgets: File directly through Corporations Canada or your provincial registry.

Accounting & bookkeeping

Pick one tool on day one and stick with it. Switching mid-year is painful.

  • Best value for most businesses: Zoho Books - deep features, GST/HST automation, and a free plan.
  • Best for inventory or product: Xero - cleaner multi-currency, better inventory.
  • Best free: Wave - Toronto-built, free bookkeeping, paid payroll.
  • Best for freelancers: FreshBooks - invoicing-first UX.

See the accounting software comparison.

Business banking

Don’t run business transactions through a personal account - CRA expects clean separation, and your bookkeeper will thank you.

  • Best low-fee: Wealthsimple Business - no monthly fee, integrated cards.
  • Best for cross-border: Wise Business - multi-currency accounts in CAD, USD, GBP, EUR.
  • Best traditional: RBC, TD, BMO - required if you want a meaningful credit facility.

See the business banking comparison.

Corporate cards & expense

  • Float - Canadian-built spend management with virtual cards.
  • Amex Business - strongest points program for travel.

Payroll & HR

  • Wagepoint - simple, Canada-only payroll. Best for 1–20 employees.
  • Humi - payroll plus HR, benefits, and onboarding.

Legal templates

  • LawDepot - shareholder agreements, NDAs, contractor agreements. Cheaper than a lawyer for boilerplate; not a substitute for one on anything material.

CRM

  • HubSpot - free tier is genuinely useful; the upsell curve is steep.
  • Pipedrive - the simplest pipeline-first CRM.
  • monday.com - flexible if you need projects + CRM in one place.

What we don’t cover

We deliberately stay out of categories where we can’t add useful independent analysis - investment advisors, insurance, and most franchise opportunities. For those, talk to a licensed professional in your province.
