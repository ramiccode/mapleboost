How to incorporate in British Columbia

BC incorporation is governed by the Business Corporations Act and filed through BC Registries and Online Services. Total government cost is around $350 including the Name Request. Most filings are processed within a few business days.

Updated June 2026.

BC vs. federal

BC corporations work well for businesses operating primarily on the West Coast. BC also has no Canadian-resident director requirement, which makes it popular with founders relocating from outside Canada. Federal incorporation gives nationwide name protection but requires extra-provincial registration in BC anyway.

Step 1: Submit a Name Request

Unlike Ontario or federal, BC uses a Name Request (NR) instead of NUANS. Submit up to three proposed names through BC Registries; an examiner reviews them and approves one (or rejects all three). Cost: $30, processing takes 1–3 weeks for standard, ~$100 for priority (1–2 business days).

Approved name is reserved for 56 days. You can skip the NR by incorporating as a numbered company (e.g., BC1234567 Ltd.) and registering a trade name later.

Legal endings: Limited, Ltd., Incorporated, Inc., Corporation, Corp.

Step 2: Prepare incorporation documents

You’ll need:

  • Notice of Articles - sets out share structure, restrictions, and registered/records office.
  • Articles - the internal rulebook of the corporation (governance, share rights). Most small businesses use a standard template.
  • Incorporation Agreement - signed by each incorporator, listing the shares each will take.

Step 3: File the Incorporation Application

File online through BC Registries and Online Services. You’ll upload your Notice of Articles and attest to the Incorporation Agreement. Provide:

  • Approved name (or request a numbered company).
  • Registered office and records office in BC.
  • Names and addresses of first directors.
  • Effective date.

Government fee: $351.58 (filing). Online filings are usually approved within 1–2 business days.

Step 4: After approval

  1. Receive your Certificate of Incorporation and incorporation number.
  2. Issue initial shares and update the central securities register (kept at your records office).
  3. Hold the organizational meeting - appoint officers, approve bylaws, set fiscal year.
  4. Get a CRA Business Number. Guide.
  5. Register for PST with BC if you sell taxable goods/services in BC. PST is administered separately from GST. Threshold to register is generally low ($10,000 in some cases).
  6. Register for GST/HST. Guide.
  7. Open a business bank account.

Ongoing requirements

  • Annual Report filed within 2 months of the anniversary of incorporation. Fee: ~$43.39.
  • Transparency Register - BC requires private companies to maintain a register of “significant individuals” (25%+ ownership/control). Kept internally but must be available on request.
  • T2 corporate tax annually.

FAQ

How long does the Name Request take?

Standard is 1–3 weeks. Priority service ($100) is usually 1–2 business days.

Do I need a BC address?

Yes - registered office and records office must be in BC. A registered office service handles this if you’re outside BC.

What’s the difference between Notice of Articles and Articles?

Notice of Articles is the public-facing summary filed with the registry (share structure, restrictions). Articles are the full internal rulebook, kept at your records office and provided when shareholders or directors ask.
